E-Bike Maintenance Guide

Keep your electric bike in perfect condition with our comprehensive maintenance guides and troubleshooting tips.

Battery Care & Maintenance

Daily Care Tips:

  • Store your bike in a cool, dry place
  • Avoid extreme temperatures (below 32¬∞F or above 104¬∞F)
  • Don't leave battery completely drained for extended periods
  • Clean battery contacts regularly with a dry cloth

Charging Best Practices:

  • Charge after each ride, even if battery isn't empty
  • Use only the manufacturer's charger
  • Allow battery to cool before charging
  • Unplug charger once battery is full

Troubleshooting Common Issues:

Battery not holding charge:

This often indicates battery age or temperature damage. Try a full discharge and recharge cycle. If problem persists, contact a certified repair shop.

Error codes on display:

Each manufacturer has different codes. Check your manual or contact us for a troubleshooting guide specific to your bike model.

Regular Mechanical Maintenance

Weekly Checks:

  • Tire pressure (check manufacturer specifications)
  • Brake functionality and pad wear
  • Chain lubrication and cleanliness
  • All bolts and connections are tight

Monthly Maintenance:

  • Deep clean the bike frame and components
  • Inspect brake cables and housing
  • Check derailleur adjustment
  • Examine tires for wear and embedded objects

Professional Service Schedule:

  • Every 6 months: Complete tune-up with certified mechanic
  • Annually: Battery health assessment
  • As needed: Software updates for digital components

Safety Guidelines & Tips

Your safety is our top priority. Follow these essential guidelines for safe and enjoyable e-bike riding.

Essential Safety Gear

Helmet (Required)

Always wear a properly fitted, certified bicycle helmet. Replace every 3-5 years or after any impact.

Reflective Clothing

Bright colors during the day, reflective gear for dawn/dusk riding. LED accessories highly recommended.

Lights

Front white light and rear red light required for low-light conditions. Consider always-on daytime running lights.

Gloves & Protective Gear

Padded gloves for comfort and grip. Knee/elbow pads recommended for off-road or high-speed riding.

Riding Best Practices

Before Every Ride:

  • Check battery charge level
  • Test brakes and shifting
  • Verify tire pressure
  • Plan your route and inform someone of your plans

While Riding:

  • Follow all traffic laws and signals
  • Use bike lanes and paths when available
  • Maintain safe following distances
  • Be predictable - signal turns and stops
  • Stay alert and avoid distractions

Group Riding Etiquette:

  • Ride single file on busy roads
  • Communicate hazards to riders behind you
  • Maintain consistent speed and spacing
  • Wait for all members at stops

Emergency Procedures

Emergency Contacts

Emergency Services: 911
EcoRiders Emergency Line: (555) ECO-HELP
Roadside Assistance: (555) 911-BIKE

Accident Protocol:

  1. Ensure your safety - move out of traffic if able
  2. Call 911 if anyone is injured
  3. Document the scene with photos
  4. Exchange information with other parties
  5. Report to EcoRiders Club for support

Mechanical Emergency Kit:

  • Multi-tool with common bike tools
  • Tire repair kit and pump
  • Spare tube
  • Emergency phone charger
  • Basic first aid supplies

Legal Requirements & Regulations

Stay compliant with local e-bike laws and regulations. Requirements may vary by location.

E-Bike Classifications

Class 1 E-Bikes

  • Pedal-assist only (no throttle)
  • Motor stops at 20 mph
  • Allowed on most bike paths and trails
  • No license or registration required

Class 2 E-Bikes

  • Throttle-assisted
  • Motor stops at 20 mph
  • May have restrictions on some paths
  • No license or registration required

Class 3 E-Bikes

  • Pedal-assist only
  • Motor stops at 28 mph
  • More restrictions on paths and trails
  • May require helmet regardless of age

Local Regulations

Age Requirements:

  • Minimum age
    • 16 for Class 3 e-bikes
  • Helmet required for riders under 18
  • Adult supervision recommended for riders under 14

Where You Can Ride:

  • Roads: All classes allowed where bicycles are permitted
  • Bike Lanes: All classes generally allowed
  • Multi-use Paths: Class 1 always allowed, others vary by location
  • Sidewalks: Generally prohibited (check local laws)

Parking & Storage:

  • Use designated bike parking areas
  • Secure with appropriate locks
  • Remove battery if leaving overnight
  • Respect private property

Insurance & Liability

Recommended Coverage:

  • Homeowner's/Renter's insurance may cover theft
  • Consider specialized e-bike insurance
  • Liability coverage for potential accidents
  • Medical coverage for injuries

EcoRiders Club Benefits:

  • Group liability coverage during club rides
  • Legal assistance for traffic violations
  • Advocacy for e-bike rights
  • Updates on changing regulations
